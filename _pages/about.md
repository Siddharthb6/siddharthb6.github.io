## Siddharth 

I am a computational chemistry researcher with a Master’s degree in Chemistry from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Roorkee. My work focuses on applying first-principles quantum mechanical simulations to understand and design functional materials for energy, catalysis, and nano-electronics.

My research experience spans molecular and periodic systems, including borane clusters, half-Heusler thermoelectrics, transition-metal catalysts, low-dimensional alloys, and perovskite–carbon nanotube hybrid nanostructures. I have worked extensively with density functional theory (DFT) using Gaussian, VASP, and Quantum ESPRESSO, and I regularly integrate electronic structure analysis with transport modelling and ab-initio molecular dynamics.

I am particularly interested in theory-guided materials design, where computational insights are used to predict structure–property relationships and guide experimental synthesis. I am currently seeking PhD opportunities in computational materials chemistry and electronic structure theory.
