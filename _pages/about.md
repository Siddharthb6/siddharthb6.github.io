## Siddharth 

I am a computational chemistry researcher with a Master’s degree in Chemistry from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Roorkee. My work focuses on applying first-principles quantum mechanical simulations to understand and design functional materials for energy, catalysis, and nano-electronics.

My research experience spans molecular and periodic systems, including borane clusters, half-Heusler thermoelectrics, transition-metal catalysts, low-dimensional alloys, and perovskite–carbon nanotube hybrid nanostructures. I have worked extensively with density functional theory (DFT) using Gaussian, VASP, and Quantum ESPRESSO, and I regularly integrate electronic structure analysis with transport modelling and ab-initio molecular dynamics.

I am particularly interested in theory-guided materials design, where computational insights are used to predict structure–property relationships and guide experimental synthesis. I am currently seeking PhD opportunities in computational materials chemistry and electronic structure theory.

## Research Experience

### Computational Studies on Borane Clusters

**Indian Institute of Science (IISc Bangalore)**
Supervisor: Prof. E. D. Jemmis

During a summer research internship, I investigated the electronic structure and stability of substituted borane clusters, [B₁₂H₁₂]²⁻ and [B₁₀H₁₀]²⁻, using density functional theory at the B3LYP/6-31G(d) level in Gaussian. Frontier molecular orbital analysis was employed to interpret bonding interactions and correlate quantum descriptors with classical bonding models in cluster chemistry.

### Thermoelectric Properties of Half-Heusler CoTiSb

**Master’s Thesis, IIT Roorkee**
Supervisor: Prof. H. Kandpal

My thesis focused on the structural, electronic, and thermoelectric properties of the half-Heusler compound CoTiSb. I performed plane-wave DFT calculations in VASP and evaluated temperature-dependent transport properties using semi-classical Boltzmann transport theory implemented in BoltzTraP2. Pressure-dependent simulations (−4 to +4 GPa) provided insight into band convergence, symmetry effects, and electron-phonon interactions influencing thermoelectric performance.


### DFT Investigation of Fe-Pincer Catalysts for Hydrogen Production

**Ashoka University**
Supervisor: Prof. V. Avasare

I conducted post-thesis computational research on iron-based pincer complexes as homogeneous catalysts for sustainable hydrogen production. Using Gaussian, I calculated reaction energetics, activation barriers, and electronic descriptors such as Mulliken charges, HOMO–LUMO gaps, and NBO populations to elucidate catalytic mechanisms and reactivity trends. Close collaboration with experimental researchers highlighted the importance of predictive modelling in catalyst design.


### Low-Dimensional and Hybrid Materials Modelling

**Indian Institute of Technology Delhi**
Supervisor: Prof. N. Ray

I contributed to collaborative projects on two-dimensional alloys and interface-engineered nanostructures. One study examined the thermal stability and electronic properties of Au–Ag bimetallic monolayers using density functional theory and ab-initio molecular dynamics in Quantum ESPRESSO. In a separate project, I modelled carbon-nanotube-encapsulated perovskite nanowires (Cs₄PbI₅ and CsPbI₃) to investigate interfacial stability and spin–orbit coupling effects on band structures. A manuscript detailing these results has been accepted for publication.
